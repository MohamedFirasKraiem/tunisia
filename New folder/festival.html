Festivals and Music of Tunisia

Tunisia is a land of celebration, where festivals and music play a vital role in its cultural identity. Rooted in centuries of tradition, Tunisian festivals showcase the country’s diverse heritage, blending Berber, Arab, and Mediterranean influences. Music, too, is a cornerstone of Tunisian life, with traditional sounds like Malouf blending seamlessly with modern genres.

Carthage International Festival

Held at the historic Roman amphitheater of Carthage, this festival is Tunisia’s premier cultural event. It features performances by international artists, blending music, theater, and dance under the stars.

Douz Sahara Festival

A celebration of desert traditions, this festival includes camel racing, folk dances, and storytelling, offering a glimpse into the lives of Tunisia’s nomadic communities.

Medina Festival in Tunis

Held during Ramadan, this festival transforms the historic Medina of Tunis into a hub of cultural activities, including music, art exhibitions, and religious lectures.

Tunisian Malouf
